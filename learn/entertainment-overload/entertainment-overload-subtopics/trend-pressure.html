Trend Pressure

Author: Darene Hanz H. Tunod

Source: https://jpt.spe.org/nodapl-and-online-activism-new-directions-effectively-engaging-critics

Trend pressure is basically a "tug-of-war" in your head. It’s that annoying fight between what you actually like and what the internet says you should like. This urge to follow the crowd is actually an old survival instinct that helps us stay connected to our friends even when we’re exhausted. Even when a trend is boring or just plain bad, we often prioritize "fitting in" over our own comfort just so we don't feel left out. This turns a relaxing hobby into a high-stakes task that feels a lot more like a second job than a break.

The Mental Cost of Keeping Up With Trends:

  • Fear of Exclusion: The deep worry that if you aren't watching what’s popular, you’ll lose your "seat at the table" in group chats.
  • The Performance Gap: Watching shows just so you can "perform" your knowledge in comments or conversations instead of actually enjoying the story.
  • Losing Yourself: Relying on the "Trending" tab makes you lose your own internal compass, which leaves you feeling empty because you aren't picking things you actually like.
  • The Trend Chase: Trying to keep up with trends that reset every 24 hours keeps your brain on "high alert" and stops you from ever truly relaxing.

The Fear of Exclusion is probably the biggest reason we feel stressed about entertainment today. Because the internet moves at light speed, the window to join a conversation is getting smaller every year. If you don't watch the #1 trending show the weekend it drops, you’re basically "opting out" of all the jokes and memes your friends are sharing. This creates a constant background anxiety that forces you to "cram" content like you're studying for a final exam. You aren't savoring the story anymore; you're just trying to survive the Monday morning group chat.

This leads to what is known as Performance Consumption. Instead of watching for fun, you’re watching to build "social currency" so you can stay relevant. You feel like you need to understand the viral references or the talk at school just to make sure you aren't left behind. When your reason for watching shifts from curiosity to just "following the rules," the recharge value of your free time drops to zero. You finish a series feeling more tired than when you started because you were working to stay current, not resting.

When you let a Trending tab pick your schedule, you slowly lose touch with what you actually find fun. Following a trend just because it’s a trend is a hollow experience that usually doesn't match your actual mood or personality. You end up in a cycle of "ticking boxes" for an audience instead of enjoying yourself for your own sake. This is exactly why you can finish a "must-watch" series and still feel bored or unsatisfied you weren't watching it for yourself, you were watching it so you wouldn't feel left behind by everyone else.

We often keep following these trends even when they’re burning us out because the "social sting" of being left out is physically painful to the brain. This constant "scanning" for the next big thing keeps your mind in a state of hyper-vigilance, which is the exact opposite of rest. To actually recover from digital fatigue, you have to reclaim your right to be "behind the curve" and choose your own path. Real relaxation only happens when you choose what to watch based on your own mood, not a social checklist you never signed up for.
