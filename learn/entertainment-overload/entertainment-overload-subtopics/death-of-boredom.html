Death of Boredom

Author: Darene Hanz H. Tunod

Source: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=q7Yyf1UNMsw

Boredom used to be a vital signal for our brains, acting as a way to tell us we needed a new challenge or just a second to breathe. Now, because of smartphones, we almost never actually feel it anymore. Every time there is a tiny gap in the day, like waiting for a ride or standing in a line, we instinctively reach for a screen to fill the void. This kind of "digital snacking" or grazing through feeds keeps the brain in a constant state of noise, which never lets it actually drop into a restful gear or process the day properly.

What Happens When You Never Get Bored:

  • Killing Your Creativity: Constantly burying boredom under short-form videos kills the mental space needed for original thoughts.
  • The Emotional Numbness: Using media to dodge being alone with your thoughts makes it harder to process how you actually feel.
  • The Attention Tax: We have trained our brains to expect a dopamine hit the second things get quiet, which trashes our focus.
  • The Content Loop: Instead of coming up with your own ideas, your brain stays stuck just reacting to random feed posts.

We have basically nuked the starting point for original thoughts and problem-solving . Boredom is usually where creativity begins, but when you are constantly burying that feeling, you lose the ability to think deeply about anything. Instead of developing your own perspective, your brain just stays stuck in a loop reacting to whatever random thing pops up on your feed next. It is like your mind is always on "receive" mode but never actually has the silence required to create something of its own.

This constant distraction also leads to a weird kind of emotional numbness . We use the media to dodge even a few seconds of being alone with our thoughts because silence feels awkward or boring now. The problem is that when you are always distracting yourself, you stop being able to process your actual emotions. It leads to a blunted feeling where you are constantly entertained but still feel empty or numb inside because you haven’t given yourself a single moment to just sit with things and reflect.

On top of that, we are paying a massive "Attention Tax" every single day. Because we have trained our brains to expect a hit of dopamine the second things get quiet, our attention spans are basically ruined. It makes it almost impossible to focus on long-term tasks, like finishing a school project or even just reading a book, because the brain has literally forgotten how to function without instant stimulation. By filling every "gap" in our day with a quick scroll, we are losing the mental stamina we need for the things that actually matter.
