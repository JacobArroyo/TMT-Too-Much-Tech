Entertainment Overload (The Fun)

Author: Darene Hanz H. Tunod

Entertainment is supposed to be relaxing but it often feels like a second job lately. With apps like TikTok and YouTube always being open there is this constant pressure to keep scrolling just so you stay updated. Instead of actually feeling refreshed most of us end up overwhelmed because of the sheer amount of content available. Even a little bit of free time becomes a headache when forty minutes of it is wasted just trying to decide what to watch.

A major part of the issue is choice paralysis. Having way too many options on platforms like Netflix leads to a type of streaming fatigue where you spend so much time scrolling that you just give up before picking anything at all. This abundance of choice makes the whole experience tiring rather than fun. Between endless podcasts and new shows it simply becomes too much to keep up with every day.

Social pressure also turns hobbies into tasks. There is a constant fear of missing out on whatever is trending which forces people to rush through a series just to stay in the conversation. Additionally the habit of multi screening or scrolling on a phone while watching a movie has really ruined our ability to focus. When entertainment becomes a mindless habit it loses its value and starts to feel like just another chore on a to-do list.
