20-20-20 Eye Rule

Author: Jazztine Kurt U. Ramos

Viewing screens has become a regular aspect of everyday living. You likely dedicate many hours to viewing screens, such as your work and home computer, smartphone, television, or various digital gadgets. Excessive screen time can cause eye strain.

A widely used approach to ease this discomfort is the 20-20-20 rule, a simple technique that promotes frequent breaks from screens or intense concentration.

Continue reading to discover more about this eye exercise, how to perform it, and if studies indicate it’s effective.

What's the 20-20-20 Eye Rule?

The 20-20-20 guideline recommends that after 20 minutes of screen time, you should take a 20-second pause to focus on something that is 20 feet distant.

Unless you possess a tape measure, you likely won't accurately measure 20 feet. Nonetheless, precision is not crucial to the 20-20-20 guideline for eye fatigue.

The key concept is that you should aim to concentrate on something distant from you. Imagine gazing out a window at something that appears distant, such as a tree or a structure on the other side of the road.

If you're in a compact area, simply gaze out the window into the horizon.

How To Remind Yourself To Do This Every 20 Minutes

You likely become engrossed in reading or tasks while using screens. Scheduling a timed reminder to appear can assist you in taking a pause every 20 minutes.

Free applications such as Eye Care 20 20 20 can also be beneficial. Simply click start when you commence your screen time, and the application will notify you to take a break.

What Does the Research Say About the 20-20-20 Rule?

The American Academy of Ophthalmology states that using digital devices won't inherently harm your vision. However, it may lead to tension and discomforting symptoms.

People typically blink about 15 times per minute. When looking at screens, the rate drops to one-half or one-third. This may result in dry, irritated, and fatigued eyes.

Eyestrain from screens is referred to as computer vision syndrome (CVS). A 2020 study discovered that instructing patients with CVS on the 20-20-20 rule resulted in noteworthy enhancements in dry eye symptoms and tear film stability.

Although general CVS symptoms exhibited only minimal enhancement, the intervention revealed significant advantages for eye comfort and well-being throughout a 20-day timeframe.

Although numerous physicians recommend the 20-20-20 guideline as the optimal protection, researchers indicate that any pause from monotonous computer tasks or screens is advantageous.

They also clarify that children usually don't perceive eyestrain as easily as adults do. Consequently, caregivers ought to keep a close eye on the screen time of children.

What Are the Symptoms of Eyestrain?

Experiencing sore, fatigued, burning, or itchy eyes is a sign of eye strain.

Additional symptoms consist of:

  • arid eyes
  • teary eyes
  • hazy eyesight
  • dual sight
  • migraines
  • discomfort in the neck, shoulders, or back
  • light sensitivity
  • trouble focusing
  • struggling to keep eyes open

Although these symptoms probably indicate eyestrain, it is wise to inform a doctor about any alterations in your vision or eye health.

What Are Other Ways To Prevent Eyestrain?

  1. Screen distance: Maintain a distance of at least 25 inches (roughly an arm's length) from your computer, and adjust the screen's position so you gaze slightly downward.
  2. Diminish glare: Think about applying a matte screen filter to reduce screen glare.
  3. Establish reminders: Utilize a timer or application to prompt you to take frequent screen breaks, such as the 20-20-20 rule.
  4. Utilize artificial tears: Purchase lubricating eye drops at your nearby pharmacy and think about using a humidifier to alleviate dryness.
  5. Blink regularly: Blinking often aids in restoring your eyes' natural hydration.
  6. Modify brightness: Lower your screen brightness if it significantly outshines the ambient light, or change room lighting to lessen contrast.
  7. Maintain clean screens: Erase fingerprints and dust, which can obscure visibility and add to eye strain.
  8. Take breaks from contacts: If you use contact lenses, wear glasses sometimes to ease dryness and irritation.
  9. Maintain lens cleanliness: Avoid sleeping with contact lenses and always wash your hands prior to putting them in or taking them out.

Takeaway

The 20-20-20 guideline is an easy and efficient method to alleviate digital eye strain resulting from excessive screen exposure. Taking a 20-second pause every 20 minutes to focus on an object 20 feet away can provide your eyes with essential relief.

Studies indicate that this practice could alleviate symptoms such as dryness of the eyes and visual strain, particularly when combined with other beneficial screen habits.

Although digital eyestrain can be bothersome, it is typically temporary and improbable to result in lasting harm. By taking regular breaks and using screens mindfully, many individuals experience relief and enhanced eye comfort gradually.
