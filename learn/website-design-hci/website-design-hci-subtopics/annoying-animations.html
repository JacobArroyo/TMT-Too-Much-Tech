Annoying Animations

Author: Heaven Lee Chryzler R. Francisco

Source: https://alyamanalhayekdesign.com/blog/bad-web-design-examples/

Animations are a common part of modern websites and apps. When used properly, they can make interfaces easier to use, guide user attention, and make the experience feel smoother and more natural. However, not all animations are helpful. Bad or too many animations can annoy users, distract them from important content, and slow down tasks. In Human-Computer Interaction (HCI), it is important to understand how animations affect users to create better digital experiences.

Animations can be useful in many ways. They can show that the system is working, help users move between screens, or highlight important elements. For example, a small fade-in animation can make a notification easier to notice without being distracting. In the same way, smooth transitions between pages can help users understand how different parts of an app are connected.

Even with these benefits, animations can become annoying if they are used too much or designed poorly. Many users find slow, repeated, or unnecessary animations distracting. Animations that take too long, move in unexpected ways, or cannot be skipped can interrupt what users are trying to do. This is even more challenging for users who get distracted easily, have ADHD, or are sensitive to motion, since too much movement can make it harder to focus.

Common ways animations become frustrating:

  • Too much movement on one screen can overwhelm users
  • Slow animations can make the app feel unresponsive
  • No clear purpose can distract users
  • Repeating animations (bouncing or flashing) can become annoying
  • No option to turn off or reduce animations can be a problem

Annoying animations do more than just irritate users. They can make tasks take longer, increase mental effort, and make it harder to focus. In some cases, they can even cause discomfort, like dizziness or eye strain, especially when animations are too fast or flashy. Interfaces with too much movement can also be difficult for users with attention issues.

To create good animations, designers should focus on purpose and balance. Every animation should have a clear reason, such as giving feedback, showing progress, or guiding attention. Animations should be fast, smooth, and predictable so they help users instead of distracting them. It is also important to let users control animations or reduce motion for a more comfortable experience.

In conclusion, animations are a powerful part of HCI, but they need to be used carefully. Poor animations can annoy users, slow them down, and affect accessibility. By using animations in a simple, controlled, and meaningful way, designers can create interfaces that feel interactive and engaging without being distracting. Good animation design helps users understand the interface better and improves the overall experience.
