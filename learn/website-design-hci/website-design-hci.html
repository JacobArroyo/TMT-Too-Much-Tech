Website Design and Human Computer Interaction

Author: Heaven Lee Chryzler R. Francisco

Source: https://www.linkedin.com/pulse/key-components-hci-pintu-d

In today’s generation, websites are part of everyday life. We use them for studying, shopping, entertainment, and communication. Because of this, designing a website is not just about making it look good, but also making sure it is easy to use. This is where Human-Computer Interaction (HCI) comes in. HCI focuses on how people interact with computers and helps designers understand what users feel and experience when using a website.

When creating a website, designers need to focus on users first. Not all users are the same, some are fast, some are not familiar with technology, and some just want quick results. Because of this, designs should be simple, clear, and easy to navigate. In HCI, the design process involves identifying user needs, creating a design, testing it, and improving it over time. This shows that good design requires continuous improvement.

One common problem in websites is visual clutter, where too many elements like text, images, or buttons confuse users. This can also lead to choice paralysis, where users do not know what to click. To solve this, designers use whitespace to make layouts cleaner and more organized. Typography and color also play a big role. Fonts affect readability, while colors affect emotions and attention, so both must be used carefully.

Modern design also considers features like dark mode and light mode, as well as accessibility for users with ADHD who may struggle with distractions. Ethical design is also important, as some websites use dark patterns to mislead users. Designers should avoid these and focus on honest and helpful experiences. Lastly, clear navigation and layout help users move through a website easily. Overall, HCI helps designers create websites that are not only attractive but also simple, usable, and user-friendly.
