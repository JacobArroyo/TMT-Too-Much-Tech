Social Media Tricks

Author: Joeshua Louis S. Villarta

Source: https://www.ncsc.gov.uk/guidance/social-media-how-to-use-it-safely

Social media apps are often designed like high-tech traps. Their main goal is to keep you looking at your screen for as long as possible so they can show you more ads. To do this, developers use "tricks" based on how our brains work. This page is an introduction to how these apps catch your attention and why it is so hard to put your phone down once you start scrolling.

One of the biggest tricks is making the app feel like a game or a slot machine.When you pull down to refresh your feed, you are waiting to see if you "won" a cool new post or a like. This, along with infinite scrolling, means there is never a natural stopping point. Your brain stays hooked on the tiny "rewards" of new content, making you feel like you might miss something important if you stop, which is often called FOMO (Fear of Missing Out).

The apps also try to control your emotions to keep you engaged. They often show you "rage-bait", posts designed to make you angry, or "echo chambers", where you only see things you already agree with. This makes the experience feel very intense. At the same time, seeing the perfect lives of influencers can make you feel like your own life isn't good enough, even though what you see online is usually filtered and fake.

Finally, some apps use "dark patterns", which are sneaky designs meant to trick you into clicking things or sharing private information you didn't mean to. This can lead to privacy anxiety, where you feel worried about who is watching your data. By learning these nine social media tricks, you can start to spot them in real-time. Instead of being "trapped" by the app, you can take back control of your time and how you feel.
