Infinite Scrolling

Author: Joeshua Louis S. Villarta

Source: https://orpetron.com/blog/infinite-scrolling-pros-cons-and-when-to-use-it/

Imagine a world where the "next" button is gone and the posts never stop. This is infinite scrolling. It is the trick social media apps use to keep you swiping forever. Instead of clicking from page to page, new pictures and videos pop up the moment you reach the bottom. It turns your screen into a bottomless well, making it easy to stay online for hours.

This design is popular because it is smooth and easy to use, especially on phones. Since you only have to swipe with your thumb, there is nothing to stop you from seeing more. It is perfect for when you just want to browse and see what is new without looking for anything specific. It keeps apps looking clean because there are no messy page numbers or small buttons taking up space.

However, this endless stream of info is a main cause of digital fatigue. Because the feed never stops, your brain never gets a signal to take a rest. This constant scrolling can be very tiring for your mind. This leads to these big problems:

  1. Addiction: It is easy to get "hooked" and spend way more time online than you planned.
  2. Slow Speed: Loading too much at once can make your phone slow down or crash.
  3. Losing Your Place: It is very hard to find a post again once you move past it.
  4. Too Many Choices: Seeing too much at once can make it hard to focus or decide what to look at.
  5. Hidden Links: Important buttons at the bottom (like "Contact Us") are hard to click because new posts keep pushing them away.
  6. Search Problems: Google sometimes has a hard time finding posts that only appear when you scroll.
  7. Difficulty for Others: People who use special tools to read screens (like the blind) find these pages very confusing.
  8. Wasted Data: It can use up your phone's data quickly without you even asking.
  9. No Clear End: Without a finish line, you might feel frustrated not knowing if you have seen everything.

To fix these issues, many websites now use a "Load More" button. This gives you the power to choose when you want to see more, which saves data and gives your brain a chance to rest. Others use "page markers" so you know how far you have scrolled. While infinite scrolling is great for keeping people busy, it must be used carefully so it does not cause total burnout.
