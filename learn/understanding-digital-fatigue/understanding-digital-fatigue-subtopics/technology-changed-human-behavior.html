How Technology Changed Human Behavior

Author: Ralph Galapin Depollo

Source: https://cleverism.com/understanding-technology-and-its-influence-on-human-behavior/

Technology has changed the way we act and how we treat each other. One big change is that we have lost our patience. Because the internet gives us what we want instantly, we get annoyed when things take even a few seconds. We now prefer short videos and quick texts over long books or deep conversations.

Social media also makes us act differently. People usually only show the "best parts" of their lives online. This makes us compare ourselves to others, which can make us feel sad or not good enough. We also lose the ability to feel what others feel because texting doesn't show a person's real face or voice.

We also move our bodies much less than we used to. In the past, you had to walk to a store or visit a friend to talk. Now, you can do everything with a few taps on a screen. While this is easy, it means we are sitting down too much, which is bad for our physical and mental health.

Lastly, we are starting to let computers make choices for us. We follow what an app tells us to buy or where to go. This means we are using our own brains less for solving problems. Over time, this can make us less confident in our ability to think for ourselves.

How our Behavior has Changed:

  • Short Attention Spans: Finding it hard to pay attention for a long time.
  • Social Comparison: Feeling bad after looking at other people's perfect photos.
  • Less Movement: Doing most daily tasks while sitting still.
  • Less Empathy: Forgetting how to understand people's real feelings offline.
