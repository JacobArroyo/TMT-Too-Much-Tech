The Google Effect (Digital Amnesia)

Author: Ralph Galapin Depollo

The "Google Effect" is when we forget things because we know we can just look them up online. Our brains have decided that it is better to remember where the information is (like on Google or Wikipedia) instead of remembering the information itself. We are treating the internet like an extra brain.

While this helps us find facts quickly, it can make us less smart in the long run. To be good at thinking, we need to have facts stored inside our own heads so we can connect different ideas. If we don't store anything in our memory, it becomes harder to come up with new and creative thoughts.

Using our phones to record everything also makes our memory worse. When we take a video of a concert or a party instead of just watching it, we don't remember the event as well. We are focusing on the screen instead of the experience, so the memory doesn't stick in our minds.

This "Digital Amnesia" means we are becoming too dependent on our gadgets. If we don't practice using our own memory, we might lose the ability to learn deeply. It is important to try and remember things on our own sometimes to keep our brains strong and independent.

Key Points of Digital Amnesia:

  • Memory Outsourcing: Letting the internet remember facts for you.
  • Weak Learning: Harder to understand deep topics without stored facts.
  • Camera Reliance: Forgetting events because you were busy filming them.
  • Brain Laziness: Choosing to search for an answer instead of trying to remember it.

Source: National Center for Biotechnology Information (NCBI). (2023). Digital amnesia: The Google effect and the future of human memory. https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC12217423/
