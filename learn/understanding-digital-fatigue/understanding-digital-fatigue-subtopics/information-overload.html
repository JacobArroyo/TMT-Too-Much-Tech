Information Overload

Author: Ralph Galapin Depollo

Source: https://www.talkspirit.com/blog/how-can-you-reduce-information-overload-associated-with-work-tools

Information overload happens when we get too much data for our brains to handle. Every day, we see thousands of posts, news stories, and videos. This makes our brains feel "stuffed," which makes it very hard to make simple choices, like what to eat for dinner.

When there is too much information, the front part of our brain the part that does the thinking gets overwhelmed. This causes us to make more mistakes and feel more stressed. It’s like a computer that has too many programs open at once; eventually, it starts to slow down and crash.

Many people try to "multitask" by doing many things at once, like chatting while studying. But the brain cannot actually do two thinking tasks at the same time. It just jumps back and forth very fast. This makes us do a worse job at everything and makes us feel even more tired.

Seeing too much news about problems in the world can also make us feel numb. When we see too many sad stories, our brains stop reacting to them as a way to protect us. This can make us feel paralyzed and unable to help or care about the things that matter.

Signs of Information Overload:

  • Decision Fatigue: Feeling tired from having too many choices to make.
  • Forgetfulness: Losing track of simple things because your mind is too full.
  • Stress: Feeling anxious because there is too much to read or watch.
  • Bad Multitasking: Doing many things poorly instead of one thing well.
