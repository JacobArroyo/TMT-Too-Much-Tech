The Rise of the Digital World

Author: Ralph Galapin Depollo

Source: https://hatchworks.com/blog/product-design/history-digital-transformation/

The world changed very fast from being physical to being digital. A few years ago, the internet was only on big computers at home or school. Today, smartphones and fast data mean the internet is everywhere we go. We are now living in a world where we are almost never truly "offline."

Companies design apps and websites to keep us watching for as long as possible. They use special math called "algorithms" to show us things that make us feel excited or upset. This keeps us glued to our screens, making it very difficult to put the phone down and do other things.

Our bodies were made for a world of movement and nature, not for a world of glowing lights and constant pings. Because technology is now part of everything we do like working, shopping, and talking to friends our brains are constantly trying to keep up with a speed that isn't natural for humans.

As more people go online every year, the line between real life and the internet is disappearing. Most people spend many hours a day on digital platforms. This means our brains almost never get a chance to be quiet or rest, which is something our ancestors never had to deal with.

Changes in the Digital World:

  • Constant Connection: Being able to go online anywhere at any time.
  • Attention Economy: Apps that are built to keep you scrolling forever.
  • Smartphones: Having a powerful computer in your pocket 24/7.
  • Screen Time: Spending more time looking at displays than looking at people.
