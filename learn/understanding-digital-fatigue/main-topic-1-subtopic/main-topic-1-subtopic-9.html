Neural Plasticity

Author: Ralph Galapin Depollo

Neural plasticity is the brain's amazing ability to change its shape and connections. This is how we learn to walk or talk. However, it also means our brains can change for the worse if we have bad habits. Constant scrolling trains our brains to have a very short attention span.

Scientists have seen that people who use the internet too much actually have physical changes in their brains. The parts of the brain used for focus and feeling for others can become weaker. Basically, your brain becomes "wired" for quick, shallow tasks and "unwired" for deep, focused thinking.

This explains why it is so hard to sit down and read a long book or have a long talk. Your brain has been trained to want something new and exciting every few seconds. When things are slow, your brain feels "bored" because it is used to the high speed of the digital world.

The good news is that because the brain is plastic, we can change it back! By practicing things like meditation, reading, or playing sports, we can rebuild the parts of the brain that help us focus. Our brains are not stuck; we have the power to train them to be strong and healthy again.

Brain Plasticity Facts:

  • Rewiring: Your brain changes based on what you do every day.
  • Short Focus: Constant scrolling makes it hard to focus on long tasks.
  • Boredom: The brain feels empty without constant digital "hits."
  • Healing: You can fix your brain by spending more time offline.

Source: National Center for Biotechnology Information (NCBI). (2020). The impact of digital technology on human brain structure and function. https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC7366944/
