Digital Dependence

Author: Ralph Galapin Depollo

Digital dependence is when you feel like you must have your phone or the internet to function. If you feel anxious, upset, or "naked" when you leave your phone at home, you might be dependent on it. This is not just a bad habit; it is a real physical need created by your brain.

Using apps and getting likes releases a chemical in the brain called dopamine, which makes us feel good. Over time, the brain wants more and more of this feeling. This creates a cycle where we check our phones constantly, even when we know it is making us feel stressed or unhappy.

Being too dependent on technology can make us feel less capable. If we always use a map to get around or always use a calculator for simple math, we lose trust in ourselves. We start to feel like we can't do anything in the real world without help from an app.

To fix this, we need to learn "digital literacy." This means setting rules for when we use our phones. Taking "digital fasts" where we stay offline for a whole day can help our brains reset and help us feel like we are in control of our lives again.

Signs of Dependence:

  • Panic: Feeling scared when your battery is low or the internet is out.
  • Phantom Pings: Thinking your phone vibrated when it actually didn't.
  • Checking Habit: Looking at your phone without even thinking about it.
  • Loss of Skills: Forgetting how to do things without using an app.

Source: Deconstructing Stigma. (2023). A guide to social media and mental health. https://deconstructingstigma.org/guides/social-media
