Online Banking Scams

Author: Ian Gabriel V. Marquez

Source: https://www.facebook.com/PhilippineSTAR/posts/banks-and-e-wallet-providers-in-the-philippines-are-facing-increasing-threats-fr/1210042931159584/

Online banking puts the ability to pay bills, check your balance, or transfer money at your fingertips. Unfortunately, it can also make you vulnerable to scammers who may try to trick you into giving them access to your account.

Internet fraud is the use of internet-connected software and online services to deceive or exploit people. The phrase "internet fraud" often refers to cybercrime that occurs via email or the internet, including identity theft, phishing, and other hacking operations intended to defraud people of their money.

Millions of dollars' worth of fraudulent activity occurs annually as a result of internet scams that target victims through online services. Additionally, the numbers are rising as more people use the internet and as cybercriminals develop more advanced methods.

There are various examples of online scams that you should be aware of. For instance, phishing is one of the most common forms of cyber fraud. In this type of scheme, online fraudsters send emails or messages that appear to be from legitimate sources, such as banks or other financial institutions. These emails normally contain links to fake websites, designed to steal users’ login credentials or financial information. When it comes to online shopping, scammers often create fake websites that might appear trustworthy, offering products at heavily discounted prices. However, once a payment has been made, the buyer receives nothing and the fraudster may even steal their financial information. Furthermore, ransomware may occur when malicious software is used to lock a user’s data and the cybercriminal demands a ransom to unlock it.

Common Online Banking Scams

  1. Phishing - A scam where fraudsters send fake emails or messages that appear to be from legitimate institutions to trick individuals into providing personal and financial information through fraudulent websites. It has become more sophisticated and widespread over time.
  2. Vishing - A form of phishing conducted through phone calls, where scammers impersonate banks or agencies to persuade victims to disclose sensitive information. Caller ID spoofing is often used to appear legitimate.
  3. SIM Swapping (“Palit SIM”) - A scam in which fraudsters gain control of a victim’s mobile number by deceiving them into giving personal details or OTPs, allowing access to bank accounts and other personal data.
  4. Smurfing - A scheme where individuals are enticed to lend their bank accounts for money transfers in exchange for payment, often used for illegal activities such as money laundering and identity theft.
  5. ATM Skimming - A method that involves using devices to copy data from ATM or credit cards, enabling scammers to access accounts, withdraw money, or make fraudulent transactions.

To avoid common online banking scams.

  1. Verify Messages - Never click links or call numbers in emails or texts contact your bank directly through official channels.
  2. Protect Personal Information - Avoid sharing sensitive details online or through messages; limit what you post on social media.
  3. Use Strong Passwords - Create long, complex passwords with uppercase letters, numbers, and symbols; change passwords regularly.
  4. Install Antivirus Software - Keep devices protected with reliable antivirus programs to detect and block malware
  5. Avoid Public Wi-Fi for Banking - Use secure networks or a VPN when accessing bank accounts or making online transactions.
  6. Be Cautious with Downloads - Only download apps or software from trusted sources to prevent malware infections.
  7. Monitor Accounts Regularly - Check bank statements frequently for suspicious activity and report issues immediately.
