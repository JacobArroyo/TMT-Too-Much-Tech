Local Cyberbullying

Author: Ian Gabriel V. Marquez

Source: https://www.facebook.com/PhilippineSTAR/posts/prompted-by-the-death-of-content-creator-emmanuelle-emman-atienza-a-bill-seeking/1314369484060261/

Cyberbullying is bullying that takes place over digital devices like cell phones, computers, and tablets. Cyberbullying can occur through SMS, Text, and apps, or online in social media, forums, or gaming where people can view, participate in, or share content. Cyberbullying includes sending, posting, or sharing negative, harmful, false, or mean content about someone else. It can include sharing personal or private information about someone else causing embarrassment or humiliation. Some cyberbullying crosses the line into unlawful or criminal behavior.

The Department of Education in the Philippines recognizes the increasing prevalence of cyberbullying. The Child Protection Policy, as outlined in DepEd Order No. 40, series of 2012, establishes measures to address and prevent such behavior. Various factors have been identified as contributing to bullying, including low self-control among perpetrators, association with peers who exhibit delinquent or antisocial behavior, negative emotional states, inadequate social skills, and low socioeconomic status. Cyberbullying is defined as a form of bullying that involves the use of communication technologies. Similar to traditional bullying, it is intentional and repetitive; however, unlike traditional forms, an imbalance of power is not always considered a necessary component.

Cyberbullying is a social disorder emanating from the development of technology and wider access to the Internet. This study aims to assess the students profiles and the prevalence of cyberbullying in the higher education institutions in Central Luzon, Philippines.

When you experience cyberbullying you might start to feel ashamed, nervous, anxious and insecure about what people say or think about you. This can lead to withdrawing from friends and family, negative thoughts and self-talk, feeling guilty about things you did or did not do, or feeling that you are being judged negatively. Feeling lonely, overwhelmed, frequent headaches, nausea or stomachaches are also common.

You have the responsibility to yourself and others to be digitally smart. Digital behavior can include reading and writing on an online forum, posting a photo to Facebook and using other social media outlets. With the wide use of social media among young people today, it is important for youth to be aware of their actions as well as those of others. They have the power to be advocates by speaking out and to help prevent cyberbullying. You can promote digital responsibility by spreading positivity and putting an end to hate by standing up to negative actions online.

Cyberbullying can have negative long-term consequences that can hinder the livelihood of children. People may not always be aware that they are cyberbullying because they are not aware of online etiquette and, moreover, the ability to be anonymous online or hide behind a computer screen can also make it easier for people to say or do things that they wouldn't do through face-to-face communication. Cyberbullying can be prevented by promoting healthy relationships and making dignity and respect core values among youth.

There are a number of steps teens can take to prevent cyberbullying.

  1. Educate Yourself - To effectively prevent cyberbullying, it is essential to understand its nature. Research the definition of cyberbullying, the behaviors it encompasses, and the platforms or contexts in which it is most likely to occur. Engage in discussions with peers about their experiences and observations to gain broader awareness.
  2. Protect Your Passwords - Safeguard your passwords and other sensitive personal information from unauthorized access. Avoid leaving credentials in visible locations or sharing them with others, including close friends. If there is any suspicion that your information has been compromised, update your passwords immediately.
  3. Maintain Appropriate Content - Exercise caution when posting or sharing images online. Consider whether the content is suitable for public viewing, including by family members. Inappropriate or explicit images can be exploited by malicious individuals to cause harm.
  4. Avoid Unknown or Unsolicited Messages - Do not open messages, emails, or links from unknown sources or from individuals with a history of online harassment. Such messages may contain malware or viruses capable of compromising your device or personal information. Delete suspicious communications without interacting with them.
  5. Log Out of Online Accounts - Refrain from saving passwords in browsers or websites for convenience and always log out when leaving a device unattended. Remaining logged in may allow others to impersonate you online, potentially causing serious consequences.
  6. Pause Before Posting - Consider the potential impact of your online posts on your personal and professional reputation. Content shared online can influence how others perceive you and affect opportunities, including employment, scholarships, and internships.
  7. Raise Awareness - Promote education and awareness of cyberbullying through initiatives such as campaigns, clubs, or events. By increasing understanding among peers, communities can more effectively prevent and address cyberbullying.
  8. Configure Privacy Settings - Limit access to your online profiles to trusted individuals. Ensure that privacy controls on social media platforms are properly configured and exercise caution when accepting friend or connection requests.
  9. Monitor Your Online Presence - Regularly search for your name across major search engines to identify any personal information or content that could be misused. Take prompt action to remove or address any content that may pose a risk.
  10. Do Not Engage in Cyberbullying - Treat others with respect and integrity online. Engaging in harmful behaviors perpetuates the problem. Set a positive example through online interactions, as your conduct impacts both your present and future reputation.
