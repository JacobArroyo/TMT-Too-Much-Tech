Live Selling Stress

Author: Ian Gabriel V. Marquez

Source: https://www.spot.ph/newsfeatures/adulting/104345/best-practices-for-live-selling-in-the-philippines-a5229-20230408-lfrm

Due to the extensive restrictions imposed by the pandemic, online shopping has experienced significant growth, and entrepreneurs have increasingly embraced internet-based commerce. Although online shopping platforms existed prior to the pandemic, measures such as travel bans have facilitated greater consumer reliance on these platforms, resulting in a rapid surge in demand for online selling. The online selling sector’s contribution to the Philippines’ gross domestic product (GDP) is projected to increase from 10% in 2015 to 25% in 2018. This accelerated growth in online shopping convenience stimulates economic activity, and the corresponding rise in GDP serves as concrete evidence of continued economic vitality.

The significance of online selling lies in its ability to address the needs of both sellers and buyers, as well as its convenience, especially given the limitations imposed by the COVID-19 pandemic. In this context, many Filipinos are increasingly turning to online selling to meet their daily needs. One key advantage of online selling is the ability to reach a global audience, which expands sales opportunities. Additionally, as many people lost their jobs at the onset of the outbreak, millions have become more interested in online selling as a source of income.

Likewise, business owners worldwide face numerous challenges as they adapt to digital commerce. Psychological research has shown that constant comparisons on social media can contribute to anxiety and severe stress. The pressure of using social media to promote a small business has led to social media fatigue among business owners, who often feel apprehensive about posting, resulting in irritability, anxiousness, and lack of focus. Small online business owners frequently interpret the loss of followers or low engagement as a disconnection from their brand, particularly due to changing algorithms on platforms like Facebook and Instagram. Surveys indicate that a significant portion of small business owners experience negative emotions, with many feeling discouraged about their business or doubting the value of what they offer. These findings suggest that social media use can have detrimental effects on the mental health and well-being of online business owners.

7 Common Live Selling Fears (and How to Overcome Them Like a Pro)

  1. Fear of Being on Camera - Feeling exposed or self-conscious. Solution: Treat live sessions like a casual video call, start with short or private videos, use good lighting, and practice to build confidence.
  2. Fear of Technical Issues - Stress over Wi-Fi, batteries, or mistakes. Solution: Prepare a checklist, test your setup, have backup products ready, and stay flexible audiences forgive mistakes.
  3. Fear of Low Engagement - Worrying about few viewers or comments. Solution: Promote your live in advance, stream on multiple platforms, start strong, and use interactive games or giveaways to boost participation.
  4. Fear of Negativity or Trolls - Concern about criticism or negative comments. Solution: Ignore or block trolls, redirect conversations positively, have a moderator, and start in smaller, safer spaces.
  5. Fear of Freezing Up or Forgetting What to Say - Mind going blank on camera. Solution: Use an outline, plan product talking points, and keep engagement questions ready; focus on connection over viewer count.
  6. Fear of Not Making Money - Worrying sales won’t come in. Solution: Use soft selling, create live-only perks, repurpose content for other platforms, and end with a strong recap.
  7. Fear of Overcommitment or Burnout - Live selling feeling overwhelming. Solution: Schedule short, intentional sessions, batch prep work, simplify your setup, and prioritize consistency over perfection.
