BPO and Call Center Burnout

Author: Ian Gabriel V. Marquez

Source: https://peopleplacesplates.ph/prioritizing-mental-health-bpo-era/

Everyday stress and burnout are commonplace for call center agents and workers of all stripes. Similar to runaway stress, call center burnout is caused by persistent work-related stress that is either poorly or completely controlled for an extended period of time.

The World Health Organization has officially acknowledged that call center agents frequently experience burnout, which is something that all parties involved should take seriously and try to avoid.

According to the WHO, burnout symptoms include a sense of tiredness and low energy, a greater mental detachment from one's work, or sentiments of pessimism or cynicism about one's work, a decline in professional effectiveness.

Both in English and Filipino, to get a broad view of what was going on. What they found was people working in BPOs faced a lot of stress, both physically and mentally, which could lead to problems such as trouble sleeping and getting sick from their work. Even though there are laws about keeping workers safe, not all companies follow them, and not many offer programs to help workers stay healthy.

Effective stress management strategies can be enacted for BPO employees Regular Training Programs, Flexible Work Schedules and Breaks, Health Support Resources, Work-Life Balance Promotion, Open Communication Culture.

It's evident that call center agent stress is a problem for the entire sector, as one study found that up to 59% of contact center workers are at risk of burnout. However, too many decision-makers may be quick to dismiss the issue as a normal byproduct of the high-performance, high-speed, and yes, even high-stress call center environment.

An analysis of the statistics on burnout and the problems it causes for agents and their employers demonstrates why the issue should not be disregarded.

Disadvantages

  • Long term stress can lead to serious mental health issues such as anxiety and depression.
  • Irregular work hours (night shifts) disrupt sleep patterns and physical health.
  • High pressure and workload can reduce job satisfaction and motivation.
  • Increased risk of employee burnout leads to high turnover rates in companies.
  • Physical health problems such as headaches, fatigue, and weakened immune system.
  • Poor work life balance affects relationships and personal well-being.

6 Ways to handle call center stress and overcome agent burnout.

  1. Build a culture that cares.
    2.
    • Create a supportive, employee-first workplace
    • Share the company’s vision to give meaning to work
    • Hold regular 1-on-1 meetings to check in with employees
    • Build trust, connection, and engagement
  2. Set clear expectations from the start.
    3.
    • Avoid confusion by being transparent
    • Clearly define roles and responsibilities
    • Maintain open communication through regular meetings
  3. Provide adequate and ongoing training.
    4.
    • Prepare agents properly and help them grow
    • Offer strong onboarding before taking calls
    • Continue training to improve skills and retention
  4. Incentivize performance and make work fun.
    5.
    • Keep agents motivated and engaged
    • Use rewards and bonuses for good performance
    • Add friendly competitions and team activities
  5. Provide the right tools and technology.
    6.
    • Reduce stress by making work easier
    • Use automation and AI to handle repetitive tasks
    • Ensure software is user-friendly and efficient
    • Implement tools like workflow automation and call detection
  6. Create a positive feedback loop of performance.
    7.
    • Boost morale through success and productivity
    • Better tools - better performance - happier agents
    • Increased success leads to higher motivation and lower burnout
