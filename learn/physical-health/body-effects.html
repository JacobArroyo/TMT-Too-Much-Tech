How Does Digital Fatigue Affect Your Body

Author: Jacob Rex A. Arroyo

Source: https://www.hrmagazine.co.uk/content/news/bad-technology-is-impacting-company-culture

In our modern world, we rarely go a single day without staring into a screen. From the moment our phone alarm wakes us up until we set it again at night, our bodies are locked in a silent struggle with the devices we depend on. While technology has made our lives faster and more connected, our physical bodies were simply not built to handle the constant, unnatural demands of the digital age.

Using screens too much and having a slumped posture can cause many physical problems. It can lead to painful eyes, neck aches, and trouble sleeping. Technology affects our whole body because it keeps us sitting down for too long, which makes us feel tired and uncomfortable.

When we spend hours on our phones or computers, we often forget to move. This lack of movement affects our heart, our muscles, and even how we breathe. This section will explain the different ways gadgets hurt our physical health and how we can protect ourselves.

We will look at common issues like "Tech-Neck," "Texting Thumb," and why headphones can hurt our ears. By understanding these risks, we can learn better habits to keep our bodies strong while still using the technology we need for school and work.
