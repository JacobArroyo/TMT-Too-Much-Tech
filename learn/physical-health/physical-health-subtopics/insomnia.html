Insomnia

Author: Jacob Rex A. Arroyo

Optional: Picture reference

Technology can cause insomnia, which is when you have a very hard time falling or staying asleep. This happens because screens give off a special "blue light" that tricks your brain. Your brain thinks the blue light is sunlight, so it stops producing the hormone that helps you sleep.

Besides the light, using technology keeps your mind too busy. Checking social media or watching exciting videos makes your heart beat faster and keeps your brain "awake." This makes it impossible for your body to relax and get ready for rest.

If you use your phone right before bed, you might lie awake for hours even if you are tired. This is called "sleep latency." Your brain is too stimulated to shut down, which means you get less sleep and feel exhausted the next day.

To sleep better, try a "digital sunset." Turn off all screens at least one hour before you want to sleep. Reading a real book or doing breathing exercises helps your brain calm down so you can fall into a deep, healthy sleep.

How Screens Ruin Sleep:

  • Melatonin Block: Blue light stops the brain from making "sleep hormones."
  • Mental Excitement: Games and social media keep the brain too busy to rest.
  • Sleep Latency: Lying awake for a long time after putting the phone away.
  • Digital Sunset: Turning off gadgets early to help the body wind down.
