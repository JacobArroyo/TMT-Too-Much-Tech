Tech-Neck (Neck Pain)

Author: Jacob Rex A. Arroyo

Source: https://hongkongsportsclinic.com/neck-pain-in-the-office/

Because mobile devices are designed to be held in our hands, our natural instinct is to lower our gaze rather than raise our arms. This forces our bodies into an unnatural, hunched position for hours at a time. What might start as a mild ache can quickly develop into a chronic medical condition as the structure of your upper spine is forced to adapt to this continuous poor alignment.

"Tech-Neck" is the pain and damage caused by leaning your head forward to look at a phone. Your head weighs about 10 to 12 pounds when you stand up straight. But when you lean your head down to text, the weight on your neck can feel like 60 pounds!

This heavy weight puts a lot of stress on your spine. Over time, the parts of your neck that hold everything together get stretched out and sore. This can lead to permanent changes in how your neck curves, causing constant pain and stiffness.

If you have Tech-Neck, you might feel sharp pains in your shoulders or headaches that start at the back of your head. It can even make it hard to turn your head left or right. This happens because your muscles are trying too hard to hold up your heavy head.

The best way to fix this is to change how you hold your phone. Instead of looking down at your lap, bring your phone up to eye level. Strengthening your back and shoulder muscles can also help support your neck so it doesn't get tired so easily.

Signs of Tech-Neck:

  • Forward Lean: Bending the neck down increases the weight on your spine.
  • Chronic Stiffness: Feeling like your neck is "stuck" or hard to move.
  • Radiating Pain: Pain that spreads from the neck to the shoulders or head.
  • Posture Fix: Bringing your device to eye level to save your neck.
