Weight Gain

Author: Jacob Rex A. Arroyo

There is a big link between using screens too much and gaining weight. This is often called "digital obesity." It happens because we aren't moving enough, but also because we tend to eat more when we are distracted by a screen.

When you eat while watching a video, your brain doesn't pay attention to how full you feel. Because you are focused on the screen, you might eat 25% more food than you actually need. Over a long time, these extra calories lead to gaining weight.

The internet is also full of advertisements for unhealthy food. Seeing photos of burgers or sweets while scrolling can trigger cravings that make you want to snack even when you aren't truly hungry. This makes it very hard to maintain a healthy weight.

To prevent this, make a "no screens at the table" rule. If you focus only on your food, you will notice when you are full and stop eating sooner. This "mindful eating" is one of the best ways to keep your body healthy in the digital age.

Why Technology Causes Weight Gain:

  • Distracted Eating: Not noticing you are full because you are watching a screen.
  • Low Activity: Sitting for hours instead of burning energy through movement.
  • Food Cravings: Seeing digital ads that make you want to eat junk food.
  • Mindful Eating: Turning off gadgets during meals to avoid overeating.
