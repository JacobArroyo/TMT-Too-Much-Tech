Texting Thumb

Author: Jacob Rex A. Arroyo

Source: https://proactive4pt.com/thumb-pain-does-it-have-to-do-with-my-phone/

"Texting Thumb" is a painful problem that affects the thumb and wrist. It is caused by moving your thumb in the same way over and over to scroll or type on a phone. This repetitive movement causes the area around your thumb tendons to get swollen and sore.

You might feel a sharp pain at the base of your thumb or a "popping" feeling when you move it. Because we use our thumbs for almost everything we do with our hands, this pain can make it hard to write, drive, or even open a jar.

If you don't rest your thumb, the inflammation can get worse and stay for a long time. Some people even feel their thumb "locking" in place. This is a sign that the tendons are too swollen to move smoothly through their normal path.

To prevent "Texting Thumb," try using your voice to type instead of your fingers. You should also use both hands to hold your phone so one thumb doesn't have to do all the work. Doing simple hand stretches can also keep your fingers flexible and pain-free.

Signs of Texting Thumb:

  • Base Pain: Sharp pain or swelling where the thumb meets the wrist.
  • Locking Sensation: Feeling like your thumb is "snapping" when you move it.
  • Overuse Injury: Caused by too much scrolling and typing with one hand.
  • Voice-to-Text: A great way to give your thumbs a rest while still being online.
