Bad Posture

Author: Jacob Rex A. Arroyo

Source: https://www.wsaw.com/content/news/The-Doctor-Is-In--509688741.html

The human body was designed to be in motion, constantly adapting and supporting itself through a balance of muscles and joints. However, our modern reliance on technology has forced us into unnatural, static positions for extended periods. When we abandon our natural alignment to stare at glowing rectangles, we slowly train our bodies to adopt a destructive shape.

Using gadgets often leads to a posture called "The Slump." This is when your shoulders round forward, your chest sinks in, and your back curves like a C-shape. Sitting like this for hours makes your stomach muscles weak and your chest muscles very tight.

Bad posture does more than just look bad; it makes it harder to breathe. When you are hunched over, your lungs cannot expand all the way. This means your brain and body get less oxygen, which can make you feel sleepy and tired.

Sitting in a slumped position also puts uneven pressure on your back. This increases the chance of having a "slipped disc" or chronic lower back pain. Your spine is meant to be straight to support your body, not curved over a laptop screen.

To improve your posture, try "active sitting." Keep your feet flat on the floor and make sure your screen is high enough so you are looking straight ahead. Using a chair that supports your lower back can also help you stay upright and healthy.

Issues with Bad Posture:

  • The Slump: Rounded shoulders and a curved back from sitting too long.
  • Shallow Breathing: Less oxygen for the brain because the lungs are squeezed.
  • Back Pain: Uneven pressure on the spine leading to long-term injury.
  • Ergonomics: Setting up your chair and desk to keep your body straight.
