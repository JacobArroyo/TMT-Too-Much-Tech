Carpal Tunnel Syndrome

Author: Jacob Rex A. Arroyo

Source: https://osteon.gr/en/conditions/carpal-tunnel-syndrome/

Carpal Tunnel Syndrome happens when a nerve in your wrist gets squeezed. In our digital world, this is usually caused by typing a lot or using a computer mouse without resting your wrists. It is a repetitive motion injury that affects your hands and arms.

Most people first notice a "pins and needles" feeling or numbness in their thumb and fingers. It might feel like your hand has "fallen asleep." If it gets worse, your hand might become weak, making it hard to do simple things like buttoning a shirt or holding a phone.

The "Carpal Tunnel" is a narrow path in your wrist that protects your nerves. When you type with your wrists bent, that path gets tight and pinches the nerve. This causes inflammation, which leads to the pain and tingling feelings.

Beyond just computer work, using smartphones and gaming controllers for hours can also trigger this issue. When we constantly use our thumbs to type or grip a device tightly, the tendons in our wrists become overworked. This continuous strain can lead to a condition where the numbness actually wakes you up in the middle of the night, making it impossible to get a good rest.

To prevent this, try to keep your hands and wrists in a straight line while typing. Using special keyboards or wrist rests can help. It is also important to take small breaks to stretch your fingers and wrists so the nerves don't stay squeezed for too long.

Carpal Tunnel Indicators:

  • Numbness: A tingling feeling in the thumb and first three fingers.
  • Hand Weakness: Finding it hard to grip objects or drop things easily.
  • Wrist Position: Bending the wrist too much while typing causes nerve pressure.
  • Micro-breaks: Short stretching rests to keep the wrists healthy.
