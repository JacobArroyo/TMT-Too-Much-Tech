Digital Eye Strain

Author: Jacob Rex A. Arroyo

Digital Eye Strain happens when your eyes get tired from looking at computers, phones, or tablets for too long. Unlike reading a paper book, digital screens glow with "blue light" and often have glare. This makes the small muscles in your eyes work much harder to stay focused.

One of the biggest problems is that we forget to blink. When staring at a screen, people blink about 66% less than normal. Blinking is important because it keeps your eyes wet. Without enough blinking, your eyes become dry, itchy, and feel like there is "sand" inside them.

If you have this problem, you might notice blurry vision or headaches. Your eyes might also feel very heavy at the end of the day. This is your body's way of saying that your eye muscles are exhausted and need a break from the bright light.

To help your eyes, doctors suggest the "20-20-20 rule." Every 20 minutes, you should look at something 20 feet away for 20 seconds. This simple habit lets your eye muscles relax and prevents long-term damage to your vision.

Facts about Eye Strain:

  • Blink Rate: We blink much less when looking at screens, causing dry eyes.
  • Blue Light: The glow from screens makes eye muscles work harder.
  • The 20-20-20 Rule: A simple way to let your eyes rest every 20 minutes.
  • Screen Brightness: Keeping your screen too bright or too dim can cause headaches.
