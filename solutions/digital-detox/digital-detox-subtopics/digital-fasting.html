Digital Fasting

Author: Jazztine Kurt U. Ramos

Source: https://gracebible.church/digitalfast/

Do you engage in doomscrolling? Are you hooked on Instagram reels? In that case, the idea of digital fasting suits you. With intermittent fasting becoming increasingly popular globally among health advocates, many individuals are also considering digital fasting for mental health improvement, as #DigitalFasting trended on X, highlighting the importance of pausing and taking breaks.

How To Practice Digital Fast

Digital fasting, or digital detox, involves refraining from using digital devices and technology for a specific duration.

During digital fasting, individuals refrain from using social media to minimize distractions and limit comparisons. They also restrict the amount of time spent on digital devices like phones, tablets, or computers. Typically, individuals disable notifications on their smartphones to reduce distractions and interruptions.

What Are the Benefits of Digital Fasting?

Most people today possess a mobile phone in their hands with a reliable internet connection. They dedicate a significant amount of time to browsing reels on social media - taking in nearly everything they find online. Social media offers a valuable way to engage with others, yet individuals frequently squander their time and become anxious as a result. Yet, exerting control over it can provide numerous advantages

  • Improved Health: Steering clear of excessive social media usage can decrease stress and anxiety.
  • Better Sleep: Individuals can fall asleep promptly if they have reduced screen time before going to bed.
  • Increased Productivity: Reduced time wastage will result in increased time for concentrated work and pursuits.

How Often Should You Practice Digital Fasting?

Certain individuals regularly opt for digital fasting; for instance, they allocate several hours each day for time without devices. In the meantime, certain individuals choose to digitally detox at intervals by stepping away from technology for a day, a weekend, or even longer.

Impact of Excessive Screen Time on the Young Generation

Diana Quadros, a Clinical Hypnotherapist and Psychologist from Mumbai, shared with NDTV, "In my role as a psychologist, I've observed the severe effects that excessive use of digital devices can have on mental and emotional health." It can result in sensations of isolation, lowered self-worth, and a feeling of detachment from our surroundings.

"Our kids are particularly at risk, as too much screen time is associated with slower cognitive growth and a higher likelihood of ADHD." "Now is the moment for us to pause, reevaluate our connection with technology, and focus on our health," Quadro stated.

A report released by the US government in 2023 emphasized that excessive dependence on screen media has spurred significant public health concerns regarding children as young as two to three years old, as it could negatively impact their cognitive, language, and social-emotional development.

The research named 'Impact of Excessive Screen Time on Child Development: A Revised Review and Management Strategies' appeared in the National Library of Medicine.

The research observed that modern technologies are now integrated into a young child's everyday existence, with children today being termed "digital natives" as they enter a landscape enhanced by mobile media that evolves constantly.

The research emphasized that social media and screen technology can enhance education and learning; however, "excessive screen time and juggling multiple media has been linked to poorer executive functioning and academic outcomes."
