Notification Batching

Author: Jazztine Kurt U. Ramos

Source: https://www.myshyft.com/blog/batch-notification-options/

Notification batching is a digital wellbeing approach that entails consolidating incoming alerts, emails, and messages to be sent at predetermined, scheduled times instead of immediately. Rather than a relentless flow of distractions, this strategy allows users to interact with digital content deliberately and selectively, alleviating the "always-on" pressure that leads to digital exhaustion.

How Notification Batching Works

Rather than responding to multiple daily notifications right away, you establish designated "check" times—such as 9 AM, 1 PM, and 4 PM—to handle all outstanding messages.

  1. Aggregated Summaries
    • Instead of five distinct notifications for five new comments, batching delivers a singular summary of updates.
  2. Focus Mode / Do Not Disturb
    • Devices are programmed to retain notifications during work hours, only delivering them during scheduled breaks.
  3. Asynchronous Communication
    • This method fosters a communication culture that doesn’t require an instant reply, enabling teams to participate at their own pace.

How It Helps With Digital Fatigue

Notification fatigue refers to a mental burnout that arises from an excessive number of low-priority, non-actionable, or incessant alerts, frequently leading to anxiety, decreased productivity, and feelings of being inundated. Batching directly addresses this by:

  1. Minimizing Cognitive Load & “Switch Costs”
    • On average, the brain takes 23 minutes to return to full concentration after being interrupted. Batching minimizes context-switching, facilitating greater concentration and alleviating the mental fatigue of frequently changing focus between different tasks.
  2. Lowering Stress and Anxiety
    • Studies indicate that grouping notifications to three occasions daily enhances users' attention, productivity, mood, and sense of control over their phones.
  3. Reducing “FoMO” (Fear of Missing Out)
    • Ironically, having fewer notifications decreases the worry of overlooking something crucial since you have confidence that you'll review everything at a designated time, instead of perpetually sensing that you could be missing something.
  4. Turning Reactive Behavior into Proactive Action
    • Batching transfers the responsibility from the algorithm to the user, allowing them to choose when to engage with the device instead of having their attention "taken over".

Practical Implementation Tips

  1. Use Scheduled Summaries
    • Activate native features such as "Scheduled Summary" on iOS or "Notification Digest" on Android.
  2. Turn Off Non-Essential Alerts
    • Turn off alerts for social media, "likes," or non-urgent updates to immediately lessen the distractions.
  3. Create “Focus” Blocks
    • Dedicate 50–90 minute segments for focused work with all non-essential notifications muted.
  4. Inform Your Network
    • Inform colleagues or team members that you will be checking messages at designated times to set expectations regarding your response time.
