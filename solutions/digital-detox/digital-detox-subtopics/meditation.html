Meditation

Author: Jazztine Kurt U. Ramos

Source: https://workscounselingcenter.com/meditation-strength-training-for-the-mind/

Meditation aids in reducing tech fatigue—a condition of mental tiredness, diminished concentration, and heightened anxiety brought on by continuous screen exposure and overwhelming information—by conditioning the brain to transition from a reactive mode to a tranquil, present-centered mode. Consistent engagement in meditation and mindfulness may lower cortisol levels, enhance attention spans, and assist in handling stress caused by digital overwhelm, as research indicates that merely 13 minutes of daily meditation over eight weeks can notably boost cognitive function and decrease fatigue.

Here is how meditation helps combat specific aspects of tech fatigue:

Reduces Compulsive Screen Usage

Mindfulness meditation aids in interrupting the "autopilot" tendency of regularly checking notifications, which may lead to a 60% decrease in automatic phone checking within eight weeks.

Improves Focus and Memory

Meditation serves as a mental exercise that fortifies the brain’s attention mechanisms, improving focus on individual tasks (monotasking) and alleviating the mental exhaustion brought on by over multitasking.

Calms the Nervous System

Always being connected activates the amygdala (the brain's stress center), resulting in a "fight-or-flight" response. Meditation reduces cortisol levels and stimulates the parasympathetic nervous system, relieving stress.
