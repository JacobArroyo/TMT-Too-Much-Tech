App Blockers

Author: Jazztine Kurt U. Ramos

Source: https://juliety.com/android-app-blockers

In the present day, with constant distractions constantly present, maintaining focus on tasks you wish to complete is challenging. Technology is wonderful, yet it can be challenging to concentrate when there are screens all around you. Our smartphones act as a virtual caregiver, hindering our ability to make time for other activities. Whether you’re doing remote work or attending virtual classes, it can be challenging to maintain focus with numerous distractions surrounding us. Fortunately, an app blocker allows us to enjoy tranquility without abandoning our digital existence! Given our current level of busyness and the numerous distractions around us, app blockers can assist you in staying focused on your objectives by preventing interruptions.

Here are the methods an app blocker can enhance your daily life:

App Blockers Help To Focus and Increase Productivity

It's well known that having too many apps on your phone can negatively impact your productivity and concentration. Utilizing an app blocker aids in maintaining focus by assisting in controlling the duration spent on applications. It also stops you from being inclined to open an app unconsciously. To keep you concentrated during the day, an app blocker can restrict apps when you aim to focus or enhance productivity. In this manner, you will avoid being sidetracked by alerts that appear every thirty minutes or so. App blockers are effective since they enable users to specify a duration for which each app will be restricted from use before it can be accessed again (e.g., one hour).

App Blockers Improve Your Mental Health

We are all at fault for spending far too much time on our devices. It can be simple to become ensnared in a social media vortex and forget the passage of time. This ongoing interaction with our phones is beginning to adversely affect our mental health in a significant manner. Individuals who frequently use social media platforms such as Instagram often perceive their lives as less fulfilling compared to those of their favorite influencers. This perception eventually results in self-judgment and discontent with their own lives and self-image. Any feeling of well-being is probably affected as these negative thoughts fill our minds. Social media also influences concentration. Currently, we often scroll through Twitter while watching television and listening to music.

You’ll Sleep Better

Research conducted in 2016 revealed that individuals who used social media apps before sleeping experienced longer periods to fall asleep, had a higher chance of waking during the night, and reported lower sleep satisfaction compared to those who avoided social media. This is logical since the blue light released by our phones has been proven to lower melatonin levels in our bodies, the hormone that makes us feel sleepy. Therefore, when we check our phones just before going to sleep, our body fails to recognize that it ought to get ready for rest.

However, using an app blocker will lead to improved sleep. It's obvious that nobody can enjoy a restful night's sleep while staying up late browsing through their social media feeds. Limiting your blue light exposure and taking breaks from social media apps like Instagram or Facebook while unwinding before sleep can help you fall asleep more quickly and maintain sleep for a longer duration.
